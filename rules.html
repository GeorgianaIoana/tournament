Tournament Guidelines

Rules of the Game

Everything you need to know about tournament regulations, time controls, and fair play standards.

General Rules Time Controls Pairings & Standings Player Conduct Equipment Appeals & Disputes Prizes & Tiebreaks
01

General Rules

The Nordic Chess Open follows the official FIDE Laws of Chess, with additional tournament-specific regulations outlined below.

1.1 Eligibility

The tournament is open to all players with a valid FIDE ID. Players without a FIDE ID may register but will need to obtain one before the tournament begins. Age restrictions apply only to the Youth Championship category.

1.2 Registration

All participants must complete online registration and pay the entry fee at least 48 hours before the first round. Late registrations may be accepted at the discretion of the Chief Arbiter with an additional fee.

1.3 Rounds & Schedule

The tournament consists of 9 rounds played over 8 days. Players are expected to be present at the board when the round starts. A player who arrives more than 30 minutes late forfeits the game.

1.4 FIDE Rated

All games in the Grandmaster Open and Challenger Series are FIDE rated. The Youth Championship is nationally rated with FIDE rating implications for eligible games.

02

Time Controls

Different time controls apply to each tournament category to ensure appropriate pacing and FIDE rating requirements.

Grandmaster Open

90 min + 30 sec

90 minutes for the first 40 moves, then 30 minutes for the rest of the game, with 30 seconds increment from move 1.

Challenger Series

90 min + 30 sec

Same classical time control as the Grandmaster Open to maintain FIDE rating validity.

Youth Championship

60 min + 30 sec

60 minutes for the entire game with 30 seconds increment from move 1.

Blitz Championship

3 min + 2 sec

3 minutes per player with 2 seconds increment per move.

03

Pairings & Standings

3.1 Swiss System

The tournament uses the Swiss pairing system. Pairings will be made using the official FIDE Dutch pairing rules and generated through approved pairing software.

3.2 Byes

Half-point byes are available upon request for a maximum of 2 rounds. Requests must be submitted at least 24 hours before the round. No byes are permitted in the final three rounds.

3.3 Withdrawals

Players who withdraw from the tournament must notify the tournament office. Players who do not appear for two consecutive rounds without notice will be automatically withdrawn.

3.4 Standings Publication

Updated standings will be published after each round on the tournament website and displayed at the venue. Preliminary pairings for the next round will be posted within 30 minutes of the last game finishing.

04

Player Conduct

4.1 Fair Play

All players must adhere to the FIDE Code of Ethics and Fair Play regulations. Any form of cheating, including the use of electronic assistance, will result in immediate expulsion and reporting to FIDE.

4.2 Electronic Devices

Mobile phones and electronic devices must be switched off and stored away from the playing area. Smartwatches are not permitted. Violation results in immediate loss of the game.

4.3 Dress Code

Smart casual attire is expected. Players wearing inappropriate clothing may be asked to change before being allowed to play.

4.4 Silence

The playing hall is a quiet zone. Spectators and players must maintain silence. Analysis and discussion of ongoing games is strictly prohibited in the playing area.

⚠

Anti-Cheating Measures

Random security checks may be conducted. All players consent to anti-cheating monitoring including game analysis and, if requested, physical screening.

05

Equipment

5.1 Provided Equipment

The organizers provide all chess sets, boards, and digital clocks. Standard Staunton pieces (King height 95mm) and wooden boards with 55mm squares are used throughout.

5.2 Scoresheet

Players must record moves on the official scoresheets provided. Carbon copy scoresheets are mandatory in the Grandmaster Open. Electronic notation devices may be used with prior approval.

5.3 Personal Items

Players may bring their own pens, water bottles (non-metallic, transparent), and necessary medication. All other items must be stored in the designated areas.

06

Appeals & Disputes

6.1 Arbiter Decisions

The Chief Arbiter has final authority on all matters during the tournament. Arbiter decisions may be appealed to the Appeals Committee within 30 minutes of the decision.

6.2 Appeals Committee

The Appeals Committee consists of three members: two Grandmasters and one FIDE arbiter not involved in the disputed decision. Their ruling is final.

07

Prizes & Tiebreaks

7.1 Prize Distribution

Prizes are awarded based on final standings after all tiebreaks are applied. A player can only receive one prize (the highest applicable). Prizes are paid via bank transfer within 30 days of tournament conclusion.

7.2 Tiebreak Order

In case of equal points, the following tiebreaks are applied in order:

  1. Direct encounter (if all tied players have played each other)
  2. Buchholz Cut 1 (excluding the lowest opponent's score)
  3. Buchholz Total
  4. Sonneborn-Berger
  5. Number of wins
  6. Drawing of lots

7.3 Special Prizes

Additional prizes for best game, best female player, best junior, and best senior are awarded by a committee. These prizes are independent of placing prizes.
